01 · Our mission

Building on the original thesis.

The first year of AI@GSB set the foundation: prepare every MBA to operate, build, and lead in a world reshaped by AI. Year two extends that work, driving toward four outcomes across students and institution.

i.

Prepare every student.

Every MBA leaves ready to operate, build, and lead in their industry, regardless of starting point.

ii.

Foster a continuous learning mindset.

The field changes faster than any single curriculum can keep up. Train classmates to keep learning long after Stanford.

iii.

Position the institution.

The GSB as the convening spot for AI in business: where leaders defining the field come to think out loud.

iv.

Raise the floor, broadly.

Optimize for breadth across the whole class, not depth with a self-selected few. Success is measured by how much the students who started furthest behind moved.

03 · What we learned

Building off the success of this past year.

Worked well · keep
  • The three-pillar frame. Foundational Skills, Future of Industries & Roles, AI Perspectives.
  • Big-name perspectives. Anchor talks drove external signal.
  • Hands-on workshops. Classmates who came shifted from watcher to builder.
  • AI Scholars as a team. The faculty + scholar pairing model is core to running this as a Dean's initiative.
Adding this year
  • Scale up the builder track. Three workshops, mandatory tooling, deploy session at the end.
  • Add a peer-demo loop. Classmate demos was the highest-signal new format last year. Making it recurring.
  • Reactive programming. Ad-hoc real-world event slot to respond to what's happening in the field.
  • Digital-first. Better website, content library, alumni-facing artifacts.

05 · How we measure

Measure what matters.

Two axes. What's changing for students, what's changing for the institution. Baseline after fall; year-end in spring.

Signal i.

Student surveys

How prepared classmates feel to lead in the AI era, measured as delta from a Week 0 baseline. We'll also run TALK-style in-room pulses at events, and a post-graduation survey of the '26 class once they're working.

Ask of administration: we'd like to push a short survey to incoming '28s before they arrive. Pre-baseline, so we can measure actual lift over the year. What's the right channel to reach the incoming cohort?

Signal ii.

Engagement

Breadth and continuity across the class, not just one-time turnout. We'll track signups versus attendance with room-count estimates, retention across the perspectives and builder series, and the share of the class reached at least once over the year.

Signal iii.

Skill application

Evidence that learning translates into coursework, recruiting, and internships. We'll watch resource-hub clicks, classmate-built artifacts and LinkedIn posts, and self-reported use in classes and on the job.

Signal iv.

Institutional position

Whether the GSB is becoming the convening spot for AI in business, visible externally. We'll watch yield rate on speaker asks (do leaders say yes when we invite them), alumni engagement with our programming, and demand from admissions and other Stanford units for our content.

06 · What we want to discuss

Discuss these openly.

Test ideas with administration before locking them in.

i.

Incoming cohort survey access.

We'd like to send a baseline AI-confidence survey to the incoming '28s before they arrive. What's the best process, and the right channel to reach them?

ii.

AAP constraints.

What are the actual limits on number of sessions, space booking, and format? Better to design to the constraints than discover them mid-quarter.

iii.

Marketing & comms support.

Admissions, alumni, and other Stanford teams have asked for digital-first content from our sessions. Is there an existing marketing resource we should partner with, or do we need a dedicated one? The single biggest unlock for institutional reach.

iv.

Summer cadence.

We'd like to propose monthly check-ins with the Deans plus bi-weekly written updates across the summer. Does that rhythm work? Goal: progress is visible and blockers surface fast, without waiting until September.
